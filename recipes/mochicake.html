Mochi Cake

This is my favorite mochi cake recipe as it uses only coconut milk to give it an extra coconutty flavor. I hope that you'll enjoy this as much as I do!

Ingredients

  • 84 grams unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 453 grams mochiko (sweet rice flour), like Blue Star brand
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • Â½ teaspoon fine sea salt
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 cups/416 grams granulated sugar
  • 2 (13.5-ounce) cans unsweetened coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon pure vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Make the mochi: Heat the oven to 350 degrees. Lightly butter a 9-by-13-inch cake pan, then line the bottom and sides with parchment paper.
  2. Whisk the mochiko, baking powder and salt in a medium bowl.
  3. Prepare the batter in a stand mixer or in a large bowl with an electric hand mixer or whisk: Whisk the eggs and sugar until pale yellow and thick.
  4. Continue whisking while pouring in the coconut milk, then the butter and vanilla. Continue whisking while gradually adding the mochiko mixture. Whisk until the batter is completely smooth.
  5. Pour into the prepared pan, then tap it against the counter to get rid of any air bubbles.
  6. Bake until golden brown and crackly, 1 hour 15 minutes to 1 1/2 hours. When you press the center, it should bounce back but not indent at all. Cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Use the parchment paper to slide the cooled mochi out of the pan.
